EUROPEAN UNION VAT DIRECTIVE IMPLEMENTATION - GERMANY

Section 1: VAT Rates

1.1 Standard Rate
The standard VAT rate (Umsatzsteuer) in Germany is 19% (nineteen percent).
This rate applies to all goods and services unless otherwise specified.

1.2 Reduced Rate
A reduced rate of 7% (seven percent) applies to:
- Food and beverages (excluding restaurant services)
- Books, newspapers, and periodicals (including e-books since 2020)
- Public transportation (short distance, under 50km)
- Hotel accommodation (excluding additional services)
- Cultural events and museums
- Medical and dental services (certain categories)
- Works of art, collector's items, and antiques
- Agricultural products

1.3 Zero Rate
Zero-rated supplies include:
- Intra-EU supplies to VAT-registered businesses
- Exports to third countries
- International transport services
- Supplies to diplomats and international organizations

Section 2: Registration and Thresholds

2.1 Registration Threshold
- Standard threshold: EUR 22,000 annual turnover (Kleinunternehmerregelung - small business exemption)
- Distance selling threshold: EUR 10,000 (EU-wide, for B2C sales)
- No threshold for non-EU businesses making taxable supplies in Germany

2.2 VAT Identification Number
Format: DE followed by 9 digits (e.g., DE123456789)
Application: Bundeszentralamt für Steuern

2.3 OSS Registration
One-Stop Shop registration available for:
- EU businesses selling B2C cross-border
- Non-EU businesses selling B2C within EU

Section 3: Filing Requirements

3.1 VAT Returns (Umsatzsteuervoranmeldung)
- Monthly: If prior year VAT liability exceeded EUR 7,500
- Quarterly: If prior year VAT liability between EUR 1,000 and EUR 7,500
- Annually: If prior year VAT liability below EUR 1,000
- New businesses: Monthly filing required for first two years

3.2 Deadlines
- Monthly/Quarterly returns: 10th day of following month (with Dauerfristverlängerung: extended to 10th of second month)
- Annual return (Umsatzsteuererklärung): July 31st of following year
- EC Sales List (Zusammenfassende Meldung): 25th day of following month
- Intrastat: 10th working day of following month

3.3 Payment
- VAT payments due with return submission
- Direct debit (SEPA-Lastschriftmandat) recommended

Section 4: Special Schemes

4.1 One-Stop Shop (OSS)
Applicable for:
- Distance sales of goods to EU consumers
- Digital services to EU consumers
- Filing: Quarterly, by end of month following quarter

4.2 Import One-Stop Shop (IOSS)
- For goods imported with value up to EUR 150
- Simplifies VAT collection on low-value imports
- Monthly filing requirement

4.3 Margin Scheme
Available for:
- Second-hand goods dealers
- Art dealers
- Auction houses
VAT calculated on profit margin, not full selling price

Section 5: Reverse Charge

5.1 Domestic Reverse Charge
Applies to:
- Construction services (Bauleistungen)
- Cleaning services for buildings
- Scrap metal supplies
- Mobile phones and integrated circuits (over EUR 5,000)

5.2 Cross-Border Reverse Charge
Applies to:
- B2B services from other EU countries
- Certain goods supplies between EU businesses

Section 6: Input VAT Deduction

6.1 General Rules
- Input VAT deductible if used for taxable business purposes
- Partial deduction for mixed-use expenses
- No deduction for exempt supplies

6.2 Blocked Input VAT
No deduction for:
- Entertainment expenses (generally)
- Gifts over EUR 35 per person per year
- Private use portion of business assets

Section 7: Invoicing Requirements

7.1 Mandatory Invoice Elements
- Full name and address of supplier
- Full name and address of customer
- VAT identification number
- Invoice number and date
- Description of goods/services
- Quantity and unit price
- Tax rate and VAT amount
- Total amount

7.2 Electronic Invoicing
- Permitted with customer agreement
- Must ensure authenticity and integrity
- E-invoicing mandatory for B2G from 2025
